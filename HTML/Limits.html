Different Kinds of Discontinuity



There are three kinds of discontinuity, removable, jump, and infinite
A jump discontinuity takes place in a piecewise function where the limit from the left and right are not equal
A removable discontinuity takes place in a piecewise function where the limit from the left and right are equal but the graph is undefined or redefined at that point
an infinite discontinuity occurs at an asymptote

Determining Differentiability from a Graph

  • Identify points of interest: Examine the graph to identify points where you suspect differentiability
    might be an issue, such as sharp corners, cusps, or points where the graph has a discontinuity.

  • Check continuity: Before determining differentiability, ensure that the function is continuous at
    the point of interest. A function must be continuous at a point for it to be differentiable at that point.
    Look for any breaks or jumps in the graph that might indicate a lack of continuity.

  • Check for sharp corners: If the graph has a sharp corner at the point of interest, it indicates a potential
    non-differentiability. At a sharp corner, the tangent lines on either side of the corner would have different
    slopes, making it impossible for the function to have a well-defined derivative at that point.

  • Look for vertical tangents: Vertical tangents occur when the slope of the tangent line becomes infinite.
    If you observe a vertical tangent on the graph, it indicates a lack of differentiability at that point.
